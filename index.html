Visit Bago City

Home of Historical and Natural Treasures

☀️

Best Time to Visit

November to April — dry season, cooler and comfortable. Avoid June–October (heavy rains).

Tourist Spots

Bago City Cathedral (San Sebastian)

Heritage · Religious

A prominent Spanish-colonial cathedral offering daily services and remarkable ecclesiastical architecture. One of the oldest landmarks in the city.

📍 View on Google Maps

Kipot Twin Falls

Nature · Waterfalls

A stunning pair of waterfalls in Brgy. Mailum, Bago City. Reach the foot of the falls via 220 steps of stairs. The water is cold and refreshing — perfect for a nature trip or family picnic.

📍 View on Google Maps

Buenos Aires Mountain Resort

Nature · Mountain

A scenic mountain resort offering breathtaking views of Bago City and the surrounding landscape. Perfect for hiking and relaxation.

📍 View on Google Maps

Rafael Salas Park and Nature Center

Nature · Park

Named after the late Rafael Salas, this nature center is a green sanctuary for families and nature enthusiasts within the city.

📍 View on Google Maps

Bantayan Park

Heritage · Park

A historic riverside park where General Juan A. Araneta led the bloodless revolution against the Spanish in 1898. Half a kilometer from the city center along the Bago River.

📍 View on Google Maps

Balay ni Tan Juan

Heritage · Museum

A well-preserved ancestral house that offers a glimpse into the rich cultural heritage and history of Bago City and its founding families.

📍 View on Google Maps

Javellana Mansion (Balay Daku)

Heritage · Ancestral House

Built in 1920 on a 440-hectare sugar plantation, this grand ancestral house was inspired by Italian architecture and designed by an Italian architect. One of Bago City's most prized heritage landmarks.

📍 View on Google Maps

Sugar Mill Heritage Sites

Heritage · Industry

Remnants of the sugar industry that shaped Bago City's identity. The Ma-ao Sugar Central and other sites illustrate the region's plantation history.

📍 View on Google Maps
Festivals & Events
FEB 19

Bugana Festival

Bago City's grand celebration marking its 60th Charter Anniversary under the theme "Better, Brighter Bago @ 60." Festivities ran from February 5–27, 2026, featuring the first-ever Bugana Festival street dancing competition, cultural shows, and community events. Tribu Mahalilum won the inaugural tilt.

NOV 5

Al Cinco de Noviembre / Cannon Festival

Commemorates the historic bloodless revolution of November 5, 1898, when General Juan Araneta led Bagonhons in overthrowing Spanish rule. Celebrated with cannon firing and historical reenactments.

APR

Panaad sa Negros Festival

A province-wide festival held in Bacolod showcasing the culture, products, and traditions of all cities and municipalities in Negros Occidental, including Bago City.

JAN

Feast of San Sebastian

The annual fiesta of Bago City's patron saint, San Sebastian, celebrated with religious processions, community gatherings, and traditional Filipino festivities.

How to Get There
✈️

By Air

Fly into Bacolod-Silay Airport (BCD). Take a taxi or van to Bago City — about 30–45 minutes. Fare is approximately ₱300–500 by taxi.

🚌

From Bacolod City

Ride a bus, jeepney, or van from Bacolod City. Travel time is about 30–45 minutes. Very affordable fares. Terminals are located within Bacolod City.

⛴️

From Manila

Take an 18-hour ferry from Manila to Bacolod, then a bus or van to Bago City. Alternatively, fly to Bacolod-Silay Airport for a faster trip.

🛺

Getting Around Bago

The city center is walkable. Use tricycles for short hops and jeepneys for longer routes. Fares are very affordable.
